Knee Protocol
Patellofemoral Pain — Root Cause Treatment

Runner's knee is almost always a hip and glute weakness issue combined with quad dominance and a tight ITB. These routines address root causes, not just symptoms.

🔥 Daily Warm-Up 5–7 min

  • Clamshells — 3×15 each side (glute med activation)
  • Hip circles — 10 each direction
  • Leg swings — 10 forward/back, 10 lateral each
  • Glute bridges — 2×20 (slow, squeeze at top)
  • Banded lateral walks — 2×15 steps each way

💪 Strength Routine 3× / week

  • Step-downs (eccentric) — 3×12 each leg ← most important
  • Single-leg deadlifts — 3×10 each side
  • Reverse lunges — 3×10 each leg (knee-friendly)
  • Side-lying leg raises — 3×15 (VMO + glute med)
  • Terminal knee extensions with band — 3×15
  • Wall sit — 3×45 sec (isometric quad loading)

🧊 Post-Run Protocol 10 min

  • Ice knee 10–15 min if any soreness
  • Foam roll ITB — 60 sec each side (not on knee)
  • Foam roll quads — 60 sec each side
  • Hip flexor stretch — 45 sec each side
  • Figure-4 glute stretch — 45 sec each side
  • Calf stretch against wall — 30 sec each

⚠️ Watch Outs

  • Avoid downhill >10% grade until pain-free 2+ weeks
  • Skip box jumps & heavy leg press on run days
  • Aim for 170+ steps/min cadence to reduce knee load
  • Shoes over 400 miles? Replace them
  • Kinesio tape on long run days offloads the patella
  • Pain >3/10 during a run = walk or stop

🏋️ CrossFit Modifications

  • Sub box jumps → step-ups (same power benefit)
  • Reduce squat depth to pain-free range
  • Avoid Olympic lifts day before long run
  • Tell your coach — they can scale WODs
  • Friday = upper body / pulling focus before long run

🌊 Recovery Tools

  • Epsom salt bath 15 min after long runs
  • Legs up the wall 10 min post long run
  • 8+ hrs sleep — adaptation happens here
  • 25–30g protein within 30 min post-run
  • See a PT if pain persists more than 2 weeks
